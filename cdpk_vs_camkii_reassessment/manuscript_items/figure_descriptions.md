# Supplementary figure descriptions

## Figure S7. Domain architecture of *Stentor coeruleus* CaMKII homolog candidates compared with reference CDPK and CaMKII kinases.

Schematic representation (not drawn to scale) of the domain architecture of the 14
*S. coeruleus* proteins previously annotated as CaMKII homologs (Rajan et al., 2026)
shown beneath two reference architectures. The reference architectures are human
CaMKIIα (UniProt Q9UQM7), which comprises a protein kinase domain followed by a
C-terminal CaMKII association (hub) domain, and a plant calcium-dependent protein
kinase (CDPK; *Arabidopsis thaliana* CPK4, UniProt Q38869), which comprises a protein
kinase domain followed by four C-terminal EF-hand motifs. Every candidate has a protein
kinase domain followed by a C-terminal array of EF-hand/calmodulin-like motifs and lacks
a detectable CaMKII association domain, matching the CDPK architecture rather than that of
canonical CaMKII. Colors: blue, protein kinase domain; light blue with hatching,
predicted pseudokinase (candidates in which the Ser/Thr protein-kinase active-site
signature, PROSITE PS00108, was not detected); gold, EF-hand (Ca²⁺-binding) motif; red,
CaMKII association/hub domain. The number of EF-hand boxes drawn per protein reflects the
InterPro member-database calls - SMART individual EF-hand motifs (SM00054) or, where SMART
motifs were not called, the number of Pfam EF-hand-pair domains (PF13499) x 2.
SteCoe_33794 is additionally flagged as a probable truncated gene model (a terminal
ambiguous residue with an incomplete EF-hand array). Full domain assignments and match
coordinates for every candidate are given in Table S19; N-terminal variable domains and autoinhibitory
junction domains are omitted for clarity.
